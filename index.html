Eindhoven's Connection to SDG 11

Affordable housing is at the heart of this vision — and Eindhoven, as a fast-growing tech city, faces one of the Netherlands' biggest housing pressures. This website focuses on Target 11.1 (Housing) to create a more inclusive and sustainable city for all residents.

🇳🇱 Housing Crisis in the Netherlands

The Netherlands faces an unprecedented housing shortage. With population growth outpacing construction, affordability has become a national crisis affecting millions.

The Scale of the Crisis

  • 401,000 homes shortage at end of 2024 (4.9% of total stock)
  • Growing to 415,000+ projected shortage by end of 2025
  • Needs 900,000+ new homes by 2030 to meet demand
  • Only 88,000 homes built annually (target: 232,000/year)
  • 7-year waiting list for social housing in many cities (national average)

Affordability Crisis

  • 5.4% rent increase year-on-year (largest since 1993)
  • Housing costs are unaffordable for millions
  • Affordable Rent Act (2024) introduced rent controls
  • Huurtoeslag (housing allowance) barely keeps pace with rising costs
  • Middle-income households increasingly unable to find homes

Root Causes

  • Population growth: 18.1M people, expanding faster than housing stock
  • Slow construction: Permitting delays and bureaucratic hurdles
  • Land scarcity: Limited buildable land in dense country
  • Rising costs: Material, labor, and land prices skyrocketing
  • Insufficient funding: Government targets not met for years

Who is Affected?

  • Students & young professionals: Priced out of independent living
  • Low-income families: Trapped in substandard housing or homelessness
  • Essential workers: Teachers, nurses can't afford to live near jobs
  • Regional inequality: Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Utrecht, and Eindhoven hit hardest
  • Tech workers: High salaries inflate housing demand in tech hubs

Why This Matters

Affordable housing is not a luxury — it's the foundation of human dignity, economic opportunity, and thriving communities.

When people lack affordable housing, the consequences ripple across society, affecting health, education, employment, and environmental sustainability.

Health & Well-being

  • Housing insecurity causes chronic stress and mental health crises
  • Overcrowded housing spreads infectious diseases faster
  • Poor housing conditions lead to respiratory issues in children
  • Displacement increases social isolation and loneliness
  • Long commutes reduce sleep, exercise, and family time

Education & Development

  • Housing instability is a leading cause of school absenteeism
  • Children have lower test scores and graduation rates
  • Homelessness disrupts educational continuity
  • Young people delay independence and career advancement
  • Brain development suffers under chronic stress

Economic Impact

  • Essential workers (nurses, teachers) leave cities
  • Businesses struggle to hire talent without local housing
  • Workers spend 40-50% of income on housing
  • Regional economies suffer from talent migration
  • Lost productivity and tax revenue

Environment & Climate

  • Unaffordable housing forces long car commutes
  • CO₂ emissions spike from suburban sprawl
  • Sprawl consumes agricultural land
  • Dense neighborhoods reduce carbon by 50-70%
  • Public transport needs local population density

Social Cohesion

  • Economic inequality widens drastically
  • Communities become less diverse
  • Public spaces empty as wealthy retreat to gated areas
  • Generational wealth gaps expand
  • Social trust erodes in segregated communities

City Vitality

  • Vibrant cities need mixed-income neighborhoods
  • Diversity creates innovation and culture
  • Without affordability, cities become sterile enclaves
  • Local businesses disappear without nearby workers
  • Resilience requires diverse populations

Our Plan: A Local Path to Affordable Homes

Eindhoven can become a European model for sustainable and inclusive housing through seven integrated strategies

These evidence-based approaches have proven successful in cities worldwide. By combining rapid construction, strategic partnerships, smart regulation, and innovative financing, Eindhoven can meet its goal of 3,000+ affordable homes annually by 2028.

Strategy 1

Build Faster, Build Smarter

Goal: Reduce construction time from 3-4 years to 18-24 months
  • Fast-track permits: Dedicated planning desk for affordable housing projects
  • Modular housing: Factory-built units reduce on-site time by 40%, costs by 15-20%
  • Office conversions: 250,000+ m² empty office space to 2,000+ apartments
  • Digital planning: BIM reduces errors and delays
  • Pre-approved designs: Skip design approval phase
Impact: +500 homes/year within 2 years
Strategy 2

Partner with Housing Associations

Goal: Leverage expertise of established housing providers
  • Woonbedrijf collaboration: Partner on 1,000+ new affordable homes
  • Co-op model: Lower costs, stronger community
  • Land leasing: Keep housing permanently affordable
  • 30-40% quotas: Affordable units in all developments (Vienna model)
  • Revenue sharing: Mixed-income projects fund affordable units
Impact: 400+ homes/year sustainable supply
Strategy 3

Smart Zoning & Land Banking

Goal: Stabilize land prices and ensure equitable development
  • Inclusionary zoning: Mixed-income in every neighborhood
  • Land banking: Prevent speculation
  • Public land auctions: Meet affordability targets
  • Density bonuses: Taller buildings for affordable units
  • Mixed-income design: Avoid stigma
Impact: Price stability + 300+ homes/year
Strategy 4

Innovative Financing

Goal: Sustainable funding independent of annual budgets
  • Affordable Homes Fund: €50-100M seed capital
  • Green bonds: EU climate finance + pension funds
  • Revolving funds: Self-sustaining model
  • Impact fees: Luxury projects fund affordable housing
  • Tax incentives: Reduced VAT (6% vs 21%)
Impact: €200-300M for 1,000+ homes
Strategy 5

Short-Term Relief (2025-2026)

Goal: Immediate housing while major projects develop
  • Startblok model: Temporary modular communities
  • Expanded huurtoeslag: Increase housing allowance
  • Rent caps: €1.5-2/m² per month ceiling
  • Community land trusts: Permanent affordability
  • Shared housing: Connect empty rooms
Impact: Relief for 500+ within 6 months
Strategy 6

Community-Led Housing

Goal: Empower residents to shape their neighborhoods
  • Co-housing: Shared spaces reduce costs by 20-30%
  • Tiny homes: 30-40m² units (€150-200/month)
  • Community ownership: Resident decision-making
  • Urban gardening: Green spaces & mental health
  • Participatory design: Increased belonging
Impact: 200+ homes + social fabric
Strategy 7

Track Progress & Stay Transparent

Linking to UN SDG 11 Targets

Eindhoven's plan directly addresses all seven SDG 11 targets, creating an integrated approach to sustainable cities and communities

11.1 – Affordable Housing

UN Target: Ensure access for all to adequate, safe and affordable housing
Current Challenge:
  • 1.6-3B people spend >30% income on housing
  • Eindhoven: 5-8 years wait for affordable homes
  • Only 25% of new builds are affordable
Eindhoven's Actions:
  • Build 3,000+ homes/year by 2028
  • Achieve 40% affordability quota
  • Reduce rent-to-income to <25%
  • Cut waiting time to <3 years
  • Convert 250,000 m² offices
Outcome: Housing accessible to all income levels

11.2 – Sustainable Transport

UN Target: Provide safe, affordable, accessible transport systems
Current Challenge:
  • Expensive housing forces car commutes
  • Only 60% have convenient transit access
  • Sprawl increases emissions
Eindhoven's Actions:
  • 70% housing within 500m of transit
  • Expand tram/bus to new areas
  • +50 km protected bike lanes
  • Free transit for low-income residents
Outcome: Reduced commutes, lower emissions, healthier communities

11.3 – Inclusive Urbanization

UN Target: Inclusive and sustainable urbanization planning
Current Challenge:
  • Urban expansion excludes marginalized groups
  • Top-down planning ignores residents
  • Growing segregation in cities
Eindhoven's Actions:
  • Community co-design neighborhoods
  • Monthly public consultations
  • Mandatory mixed-income integration
  • Housing access for vulnerable groups
Outcome: Communities shape neighborhoods, stronger cohesion

11.4 – Cultural Heritage

UN Target: Protect cultural and natural heritage
Current Challenge:
  • Development destroys historic areas
  • Gentrification erases identity
  • Lost cultural landmarks
Eindhoven's Actions:
  • Preserve Philips factory buildings
  • Adaptive reuse of landmarks
  • Community archives & history projects
  • Protect heritage neighborhoods
Outcome: Culturally-rich neighborhoods honoring history

11.5 – Disaster Resilience

UN Target: Reduce deaths and losses from disasters
Current Challenge:
  • Billions in climate disaster damage
  • Flood & heat risks increasing
  • Vulnerable populations most affected
Eindhoven's Actions:
  • Climate-resilient housing designs
  • Green infrastructure (rain gardens)
  • Cool roofs & passive cooling
  • Emergency response training
Outcome: Communities safe from climate impacts

11.6 – Environmental Impact

UN Target: Reduce environmental impact, air quality, waste
Current Challenge:
  • Air pollution: 7M+ deaths annually
  • Buildings: 39% of global emissions
  • Municipal waste overwhelms landfills
Eindhoven's Actions:
  • 100% renewable energy in new homes
  • Net-zero carbon standards
  • Circular economy programs
  • EV charging in all projects
Outcome: Clean air, zero-carbon neighborhoods

11.7 – Green & Public Spaces

UN Target: Universal access to safe, green public spaces
Current Challenge:
  • Only 3.2% urban land is green space
  • Poor neighborhoods lack parks
  • Nature deficit affects health
Eindhoven's Actions:
  • Increase to 15 m² per capita
  • Parks within 10 min walk of all homes
  • Community gardens in every area
  • Urban forests & biodiversity
Outcome: Healthy neighborhoods with integrated nature

Interview: A Student's Struggle

Housing Search Process

"Half of the websites are scammers, and the other half is already rented out. There's no real person to talk to—it's all websites. The whole process is completely impersonal and frustrating."

Daily Life Impacts

"I can't have a social life. The housing search takes a big part of my day, and I have to spend a lot of money on public transport just to get around. I can't cook daily because of my living situation. There are no affordable sports facilities in my area, and I simply cannot have fun like I used to."

Housing Conditions

"You cannot choose your roommates. You're not even sure if the home will be in decent condition when you move in. It's a gamble every time."

Importance of Staying in Eindhoven

"It's 9 out of 10 important for me to stay here. All of my friends are from this city, so leaving would mean leaving my entire social network behind."

Rental Services

"The subscription services for housing platforms are really expensive, and they're time-limiting. You pay a lot of money just for the chance to apply for a place you might not even get."

Anonymous Eindhoven Student (2025)
